Strength Training

Squat

Squat is one of the purest strength training Exercises
as they work on more than one muscles than any other exercises Many of these muscles help power you through daily tasks such as walking, climbing stairs, bending, or carrying heavy loads. Adding squats to your workouts can help boost your exercise performance, decrease your risk of injury, and keep you moving more easily throughout the day

Dead-lift

When it comes down to the fitness and bodybuilding world the deadlift is an exercise you do not want to ignore The deadlift is an exercise that requires to literally lift a dead weight off the floor It has been around for ages, performed by many known athletes to improve their athletic abilities as well as their performance and gain a good amount of strength and size. It is a full-body blaster which is one of the top advantages of this exercise

Glute Bridge

Glute training is key for increasing strength, power, and performance While many strength, power, and fitness athletes spend most of their weight training performing squats, deadlifts, pressing, Olympic lifts, and more, most athletes and coaches integrate movements like the glute bridge within a training program to address muscle weaknesses or as a corrective exercise. Knee and lower back pain is common while training for strength Stronger glutes can relieve spinal extension stress, help to fully extend the hips, and can act as an antagonist during squatting movements to help stabilize and balance out the forces on the knee.

Push-Up

Moving your own bodyweight is one of the best signs of strength The push-up works all the pressing muscles in the upper body, including your chest, shoulders, and triceps, and can help you get stronger in the dumbbell or barbell chest press.

Bent-Over Row

This exercise uses all the pulling muscles in your upper body, including the back, shoulders, and biceps Working on increasing your rowing strength can also help you get to your first bodyweight pull-up—a challenging exercise that’s also a great indicator of strength

Lunges

Lunges are a popular strength training exercise among people wanting to strengthen, sculpt, and tone their bodies, while also improving overall fitness and enhancing athletic performance.
This resistance exercise is popular for its ability to strengthen your back, hips, and legs, while improving mobility and stability. Lunges are ideal for those wishing to get stronger and for current athletes, including runners and cyclists.
