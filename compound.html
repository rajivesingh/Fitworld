Compound-Exercises

Compound exercises are exercises that work on different muscle groups at the same time. For example squat dead lifts Dumbbell shoulder press
Compound exercises differ from isolation exercises. these exercises works on a single muscle group at a time.
For example bicep curl is an isolation exercise meant to strengthen the biceps

Benifts of Compound-Exercises

One of the biggest beifts of compound exercises is you work on more than one muscles and build more strength by focusing on compound exercises.

  • Burns out more calories
  • Improving strength
  • Targeting multiple muscle groups in less time
  • Allow lifting heavier loads
  • Improves balance

Bellow we have listed 5 Compound-Exercises that you can include in your next workout

Barbell Bench Press

  • Lie down on your back on a flat bench
  • Place your legs on side of the bench
  • Grab the bar with both hands with a wide angle
  • Take a deep breath. Draw the bar down until it touches your chest
  • Breath out as you take the bar away from your chest
  • repeat 8-10 times

Barbell Deadlifts

  • Take a barbell and place it against your shins
  • Bend your hips and keens to grab the bar with your hands just beyound the shoulders width
  • keeping your lower back arched
  • Pull your torso up and hips forward as you stand
  • Keep the bar close to your body as you lift the bar
  • Repeat 5-8 times

Squats

  • Stand with feet a little wider than hip width, toes facing front.
  • Tighten your stomach muscles.
  • Lower down, as if sitting in an invisible chair.
  • Straighten your legs to lift back up.
  • Repeat for three sets of 10 to 15 repss

Pull-up

pull-up is one of the best compound exercise that involves a large number of big and small muscles addition to working multiple muscles at the same time, the pull-up is also a multi-joint movement, an exercise that causes more than one joint to move You can practice this while standing freely or by hanging from your pull-up bar and bringing those shoulder blades in.
This movement engages your lats, the large set of muscles in your back, that are critical supporting muscles.

Shrugs

  • Hold the dumbbells by your side with your palms facing inwards.
  • Your feet should be shoulder-width apart
  • Raise your shoulders as high as you can by contracting your traps.
  • Lower the dumbbells back down and repeat.
